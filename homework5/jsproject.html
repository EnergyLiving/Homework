INTEGRATED PROGRAM EVALUATION

A Business Process Model that evaluates the accomplishments of Action Officers/Managers juxtaposed alongside the guidelines, direction, advice and assistance given by the Proponents. It is a tool which provides the organization's Leaders a real-time, incremental view of the status, effectiveness, and buy-in, of the organization's programs/ systems/ strategic plans; ensures both Proponents and Action Officers/Managers are held accountable.

THE PROCESS

;

STEP TARGET

STEP 1:  Break down the organization's  programs, systems, or plans to the lowest possible denominator:  ;Flow Through Tasks;--both mandatory and recommended.

 Clarifies the leaders' level of effort required to accomplish the mission of the organization.   

STEP 2:  Visually map the; flow-through tasks; covering the  evaluation time frame.

 Puts those ;flow-through tasks; in perspective along a finite time-line.    

STEP 3:  Post the Visual Map for program proponents, action officers, and managers.

 Provides a visual view of expectations.

STEP 4:  30-60 days prior to due date, ask addressee to  certify that the ;flow-through; task has been completed.

STEP 4-1:  With the certification request,  provide infographic with points of contact; governing policy; goals of the strategic plan, program or system as it relates to each task;  contextual statistics; business process maps (step-by-step ;how- to's;) for each task; 45 second high impact marketing video from the leadership; and a running total of the % of individuals who have completed the task.  

Gathers Real-Time Status of actions.   

Provides Real-Time Consultancy.  Provides a format for proponents to help Action Officers/Managers as well as the Leadership see the underlying reasons for completing actions and how those actions fit into the overarching goals of the system, program, or strategic plan. 

STEP 5:  Provide status of actions to the leadership weekly, bi-weekly, or as requested. 

Allows the Leadership and the Proponent to identify  ;buy-in; and/ or  ;doability; issues.  Refocuses timely and robust  internal audit programs from merely assessing compliance to assessing effectiveness of programs, systems, and strategic plans. 

STEP 6:  Post  status of actions organization-wide

 Provides transparency to the systems and programs of the organization.
